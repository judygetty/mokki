What is Functional Medicine?

Functional Medicine is a systems-based approach to healthcare that seeks to identify and address the root causes of disease, rather than simply managing symptoms. It treats the body as an interconnected whole — not a collection of isolated parts — and takes into account the complex interactions between genetics, environment, and lifestyle.

Where conventional medicine typically asks "what disease do you have?", Functional Medicine asks "why does this person have this symptom, and what can we do to restore normal function?" This shifts the focus from disease management to true health creation.

"Functional Medicine doesn't replace conventional medicine. It complements it — by asking deeper questions and looking for patterns that standard testing misses."

What is DNA Care & Epigenetics?

Your DNA is not your destiny. While your genes provide a blueprint, epigenetics — the study of how gene expression is regulated — shows us that how your genes behave is influenced enormously by how you live, eat, sleep, move and manage stress.

DNA health testing at Mökki uses advanced genetic analysis to identify predispositions — to inflammation, to nutrient absorption difficulties, to hormonal imbalances, to detoxification challenges — so we can design targeted interventions before problems develop.

"Your genes are not your destiny. They respond to how you live, rest, eat, move, and think. Understanding your DNA gives us the map — Functional Medicine gives us the tools to navigate it."

What is Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT)?

Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy involves breathing pure (100%) oxygen inside a pressurised chamber. Under pressure, oxygen dissolves into the plasma and reaches tissues that normal red blood cells cannot easily access. This triggers a cascade of regenerative responses at a cellular level.

Research from the Shamir Medical Center in Israel (2020) demonstrated that HBOT protocols can actually lengthen telomeres — the protective caps on chromosomes associated with biological ageing — while simultaneously reducing senescent ("zombie") cells. This makes it one of the most exciting tools in the longevity toolkit.

What is Red Light Therapy (Photobiomodulation)?

Red light therapy uses specific wavelengths of red (630–700nm) and near-infrared (800–1100nm) light to penetrate skin and underlying tissue. At the cellular level, these wavelengths are absorbed by the mitochondria — the energy-producing structures in every cell — and stimulate the production of ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the primary energy currency of the body.

The result is a reduction in oxidative stress, improved cellular function, reduced inflammation and accelerated tissue repair. It is one of the most well-studied non-invasive therapies in the longevity space, with strong evidence for its use in skin health, muscle recovery, pain management, sleep quality and cognitive function.
